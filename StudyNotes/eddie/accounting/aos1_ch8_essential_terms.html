💡 Cash vs Profit — what's the difference? ▼

Cash

The actual money in the bank account. Recorded when money physically moves in or out.

A sale made in June but paid in August → recorded as cash in August only.

Profit

Revenue earned minus expenses incurred — regardless of when cash is received or paid.

That same June sale → counted as revenue in June when it was earned.
A business can be profitable but still run out of cash!
Eg: Net profit = $20,000 but $18,000 is tied up in unpaid customer invoices → only $2,000 actual cash available to pay bills.

💡 This is why both an Income Statement AND a Statement of Receipts & Payments are needed.
