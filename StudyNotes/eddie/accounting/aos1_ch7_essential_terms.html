🧾 How GST Works in Accounting ▼

GST Collected (liability ↑)

When you sell goods/services, you charge customers 10% GST on top of the price. You collect this on behalf of the ATO — it is NOT your income.

Sale price $100 + GST $10 = $110 charged
GST Collected = $10 (owed to ATO)

GST Paid (reduces liability)

When you buy goods/services for the business, you pay 10% GST to suppliers. This can be claimed back from the ATO.

Purchase $200 + GST $20 = $220 paid
GST Paid = $20 (claimable from ATO)
GST Payable to ATO = GST Collected − GST Paid
Eg: Collected $800 − Paid $300 = $500 payable to ATO this quarter

💡 If GST Paid > GST Collected, the ATO owes YOU a refund.
